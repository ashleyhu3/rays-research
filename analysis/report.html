E5

Granger-style lagged regression

Regress each stock's return on 1–3 week lags of token growth (and the reverse), with Newey–West/HAC standard errors. The F-test asks whether the lag block adds predictive power.

ModelR²F p-valueBest lag (p)Read
The one model that flirts with significance is NVDA → tokens (R²=0.25, lag-1 p=0.02, joint F p=0.06): market moves weakly precede token-growth changes, not the other way round. With n≈22 and a heavily autocorrelated token ramp, treat even this as provisional — a single test that would not survive the multiple-comparison bar E1 held it to.

Method & caveats

How to read this

  • This is a 25-week pilot, not the 80-week study. The full daily rankings history (back to 2025-01-01) sits behind OpenRouter's paid dataset key, which isn't present in this environment. The token series here was reconstructed — losslessly to ~0.1px — from the exact SVG bar heights in the platform's 2026-07-10 weekly report, covering 2025-12-29 → 2026-06-15.
  • Low power by construction. ~24 weekly return observations. The plan's guardrails — FDR correction, split-half stability — are applied where possible, but small-n means "no signal" ≠ "no relationship."
  • OpenRouter is a small, enthusiast-skewed slice of total inference demand. A null here means no signal in this slice.
  • Blended price is mix-blended, not a true price series. It applies today's list prices to historical volume, so its motion reflects model-mix shift, not real price cuts. A genuine list-price history only began accruing 2026-07-09.
  • Stocks: weekly adjusted closes from Yahoo Finance, aligned to the same ISO Monday grid; the in-progress final week is dropped.

To upgrade this to the real study: drop in an OPENROUTER_API_KEY and the pipeline extends to ~80 weeks and unlocks the two experiments this slice can't support — E4 (daily event study on demand shocks) and E6 (cross-sectional token momentum).
